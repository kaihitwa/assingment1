Swami Vivekanand


"Arise,awake,and stop not until the goal is reached"

Here's a time line of Swami Vivekanand life:

  • 1863-Born as Narendranath datta in Kolkata,India
  • 1879-Education beings :Narendranath joined the Metropolitan Institution of Ishwar Chandra Vidyasagar and later attended the Scottish Church College in Kolkata, where he studied Western philosophy, history, and social sciences.
  • 1881-First Meeting with Ramakrishna : Narendranath first met Ramakrishna Paramahamsa at the Dakshineswar Kali Temple, which marked a turning point in his spiritual life.
  • 1884 - Father's Death : The death of his father plunged his family into financial crisis, making him more introspective and spiritually inclined.
  • 1886-Ramakrishna's Death: Ramakrishna passed away, and Narendranath, along with other disciples, started following a monastic life and took the name Swami Vivekananda
  • 1888 -Beginning of Monkhood & Travel: Vivekananda embarked on a journey across India as a wandering monk, learning about the country's diverse culture and the struggles of its people.
  • 1893-Chicago Address: September 11, 1893 Vivekananda delivered his historic speech at the World’s Parliament of Religions in Chicago, introducing Hindu philosophy to the Western world.
  • 1894-Establishment of Vedanta Societies: Vivekananda established the Vedanta Society in New York, the first of its kind, to spread the teachings of Vedanta and Hindu philosophy in the West.
  • 1895-Lecture Tour in the U.S: Vivekananda continued giving lectures on Vedanta and yoga in the U.S., gaining a following and spreading Indian spiritual thought
  • 1896-Visit to England : Swami Vivekananda visited England and met influential thinkers like Max Müller, and began to establish Vedanta societies in Europe.
  • 1897-Return to India: January 1897: Swami Vivekananda returned to India and was given a hero’s welcome. He founded the Ramakrishna Mission to carry out social and spiritual activities.
  • 1898- Belur Math : December 9, 1898: Vivekananda established the Belur Math, the headquarters of the Ramakrishna Math and Mission, on the banks of the Ganges near Kolkata
  • 1899-Second Visit to the West Vivekananda made a second trip to the West, visiting the U.S. and Europe to further propagate Vedanta and the Indian spiritual philosophy.
  • 1900-Health Decline : Due to intense work and travel, Vivekananda's health began to deteriorate.
  • 1902- Death : July 4, 1902: Swami Vivekananda passed away at Belur Math at the age of 39. His teachings and legacy continued to inspire people globally


    • Swami Vivekananda’s life was short but impactful, contributing immensely to the revival of Hinduism, Indian nationalism, and global spirituality.
